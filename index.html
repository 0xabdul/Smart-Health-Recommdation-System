First Aid Guide

Essential emergency procedures and life-saving techniques

Emergency Call

911

Quick Guide

DRSABCD Action Plan

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)

Life-Threatening
1

Check Responsiveness

Gently shake the person and shout, "Are you okay?" Check if they respond.

2

Call for Help

If unresponsive, call 911 immediately and get an AED if available.

3

Open Airway

Tilt head back, lift chin to open airway. Check for breathing for 10 seconds.

4

Start Compressions

Place hands in center of chest, press hard and fast at 100-120 compressions per minute.

Push at least 2 inches deep, allow full chest recoil
5

Rescue Breaths

After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths. Watch chest rise.

6

Continue CPR

Continue 30:2 ratio until help arrives, person responds, or AED is ready.

CPR Rhythm Guide

Stayin' Alive - Bee Gees (100 BPM)
Another One Bites the Dust - Queen (110 BPM)

Choking Emergency

Life-Threatening

Conscious Adult

1

Ask "Are you choking?"

If they can speak, cough, or breathe, encourage coughing.

2

Heimlich Maneuver

Stand behind, wrap arms around waist, make fist above navel, thrust inward and upward.

3

Continue Until Object Expels

Repeat thrusts until object comes out or person becomes unconscious.

Unconscious Person

1

Call 911

Emergency services immediately.

2

Start CPR

Begin CPR with chest compressions.

3

Check Mouth

Before breaths, check mouth for visible object and remove if possible.

Bleeding Control

Urgent
1

Apply Direct Pressure

Use clean cloth or gauze, apply firm pressure directly to wound.

2

Elevate Injured Area

Raise the wound above heart level if possible, unless broken bone suspected.

3

Apply Pressure Bandage

Wrap firmly with bandage, but don't cut off circulation.

!

Severe Bleeding

If bleeding doesn't stop, apply tourniquet 2-3 inches above wound. Call 911 immediately.

Burn Treatment

Urgent

Minor Burns (1st Degree)

1

Cool the Burn

Run cool (not cold) water for 10-20 minutes.

2

Cover Loosely

Use sterile, non-stick bandage.

Severe Burns (2nd/3rd Degree)

1

Call 911

Emergency medical care required.

2

Cover with Clean Cloth

Do not apply water, ointments, or break blisters.

❌ What NOT to Do:

  • Don't apply ice directly to burns
  • Don't break blisters
  • Don't apply butter, oils, or ointments
  • Don't use fluffy cotton on open burns

Fractures & Sprains

Urgent
1

Immobilize

Keep the injured area still. Don't try to straighten broken bones.

2

Apply Cold Pack

Reduce swelling with cold pack for 15-20 minutes.

3

Create Splint

Use rigid material (wood, magazine) and soft padding to immobilize.

4

Elevate

Raise injured area above heart level if possible.

!

Seek Medical Care

For suspected fractures, seek medical attention immediately.

Poisoning Emergency

Life-Threatening

Poison Control Center

1-800-222-1222

Available 24/7 for poisoning emergencies

1

Call Poison Control

Call immediately with the substance and amount ingested.

2

Follow Instructions

Follow specific guidance from poison control experts.

3

Don't Induce Vomiting

Unless specifically instructed by poison control.

!

Unconscious or Seizures

Call 911 immediately if person is unconscious, having seizures, or difficulty breathing.

Seizure Response

Urgent
1

Stay Calm

Most seizures last 1-3 minutes and are not medical emergencies.

2

Protect from Injury

Move hard objects away, place something soft under head.

3

Time the Seizure

Note when it starts and how long it lasts.

4

Turn on Side

If possible, turn person on side to prevent choking.

❌ What NOT to Do:

  • Don't restrain the person
  • Don't put anything in their mouth
  • Don't try to give food or water

🚨 Call 911 if:

  • Seizure lasts more than 5 minutes
  • Person is injured during seizure
  • Person has difficulty breathing afterward
  • Second seizure occurs shortly after first
  • Person is pregnant or has diabetes

Allergic Reactions

Life-Threatening

Mild Reaction

Symptoms: Hives, itching, rash, mild swelling

1

Remove Allergen

Get away from source of allergic reaction.

2

Take Antihistamine

If available, take Benadryl or similar medication.

Anaphylaxis (Severe Reaction)

Symptoms: Difficulty breathing, swelling of face/throat, dizziness, rapid heartbeat, loss of consciousness

!

Call 911 Immediately

This is a life-threatening emergency.

2

Use EpiPen

Inject epinephrine if available, in outer thigh.

3

Lay Person Flat

Lay down with legs elevated unless breathing is difficult.

Heart Attack Emergency

Life-Threatening

⚠️ Warning Signs:

  • Chest pain, pressure, or squeezing
  • Pain spreading to arms, neck, jaw, or back
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
!

Call 911 Immediately

Every minute counts. Call emergency services right away.

2

Keep Person Calm

Have them sit or lie down comfortably. Loosen tight clothing.

3

Give Aspirin

If available and not allergic, give one 325mg aspirin to chew slowly.

4

Monitor Breathing

Watch for changes in consciousness or breathing. Be ready to perform CPR.

Stroke Emergency

Life-Threatening

🧠 FAST Test for Stroke:

F
Face

Ask person to smile. Does one side droop?

A
Arms

Ask person to raise both arms. Does one drift down?

S
Speech

Ask person to repeat a simple phrase. Is speech slurred?

T
Time

Time to call 911 immediately if any signs present.

!

Call 911 Immediately

Time is brain. Every minute delays treatment increases damage.

2

Note Time of Onset

Record when symptoms started. This affects treatment options.

3

Position Comfortably

Have person lie on their side with head elevated if conscious.

4

Don't Give Food or Water

Person may have difficulty swallowing and could choke.

Drowning Emergency

Life-Threatening

Active Drowning

Signs: Head low in water, mouth at water level, vertical body, no forward progress

!

Call for Help

Yell for assistance and call 911 if possible.

2

Throw Don't Go

Throw flotation device. Only enter water if trained.

3

Reach with Equipment

Use pole, rope, or paddle to reach from safe position.

After Rescue

1

Check Responsiveness

Tap and shout to check if person responds.

2

Start CPR if Needed

Begin CPR immediately if not breathing.

3

Remove Wet Clothing

Replace with dry blankets to prevent hypothermia.

Electric Shock Emergency

Life-Threatening
!

Don't Touch Person

Do not touch the person while they're in contact with electricity.

2

Turn Off Power

Shut off electricity at circuit breaker or unplug device.

3

Use Non-Conductive Object

Use wooden broom handle or plastic object to move person away.

4

Call 911

Even if person seems fine, internal injuries may exist.

5

Check for Burns

Look for entry and exit burn marks. Cover with sterile dressing.

6

Monitor for Shock

Watch for changes in consciousness or breathing.

Hypothermia Emergency

Urgent

Mild (95-90°F / 35-32°C)

Shivering, numb hands, confusion

Moderate (90-82°F / 32-28°C)

Violent shivering, slurred speech, drowsiness

Severe (Below 82°F / 28°C)

No shivering, unconscious, irregular heartbeat

!

Call 911 for Severe Cases

Immediate medical care needed for moderate to severe hypothermia.

2

Move to Warm Location

Get person out of cold and remove wet clothing.

3

Warm Gradually

Use blankets, warm (not hot) drinks, skin-to-skin contact.

4

Apply Warm Compresses

Place on neck, chest, and groin. Avoid extremities.

❌ What NOT to Do:

  • Don't use direct heat (heating pads, hot water)
  • Don't massage arms or legs
  • Don't give alcohol
  • Don't try to rapidly rewarm

Heat Stroke Emergency

Life-Threatening

🌡️ Critical Signs:

  • High body temperature (103°F / 39.4°C or higher)
  • Hot, red, dry, or damp skin
  • Headache, dizziness, confusion
  • Nausea, vomiting, loss of consciousness
!

Call 911 Immediately

Heat stroke is a medical emergency.

2

Move to Cool Place

Get person to shade or air-conditioned environment.

3

Cool Rapidly

Use cold water immersion, ice packs, or wet sheets with fans.

4

Monitor Temperature

Continue cooling until temperature drops to 101-102°F.

Diabetes Emergency

Urgent

🩸 Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia)

Signs: Shakiness, sweating, confusion, irritability, rapid heartbeat

1

Give Fast-Acting Sugar

4 oz juice, regular soda, or glucose tablets.

2

Wait 15 Minutes

Recheck blood sugar and repeat if still low.

3

Follow with Snack

Once stable, eat protein and complex carb.

⚠️ High Blood Sugar (Hyperglycemia)

Signs: Extreme thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision

1

Check for Ketones

Use urine test strips if available.

2

Hydrate

Drink water to prevent dehydration.

!

Call 911 for DKA

If vomiting, confusion, or difficulty breathing occur.

Fainting (Syncope)

Urgent
1

Check Breathing

Ensure person is breathing normally.

2

Position Correctly

Lie person flat and elevate legs 12 inches above heart.

3

Loosen Clothing

Remove tight collars, belts, or restrictive clothing.

4

Check for Injuries

Look for head injuries from falling.

5

Monitor Recovery

Person should regain consciousness within 1-2 minutes.

🚨 Call 911 if:

  • Doesn't regain consciousness within 2 minutes
  • Difficulty breathing or chest pain
  • Pregnant, over 50, or has heart condition
  • Fell from height or hit head
  • Seizures occur during fainting

DRSABCD Action Plan

D

Danger

Check for danger to yourself, others, and the patient.

R

Response

Check for response by calling their name and gently shaking.

S

Send for help

Call 911 or ask someone else to call for emergency help.

A

Airway

Open mouth, check for obstructions, tilt head back to open airway.

B

Breathing

Check for normal breathing for up to 10 seconds.

C

CPR

Start CPR if not breathing normally.

D

Defibrillator

Use AED as soon as available.
